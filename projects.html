zk-ScalHard: Security & Privacy in Common European Mobility Data Space (EMDS)

Active
MSCA Seal of Excellence Inria · SERENDIPITY Team Score 88/100

A Marie Skłodowska-Curie Actions Seal of Excellence fellowship hosted at Inria, Lille, investigating how sovereign, privacy-preserving data spaces can underpin trusted mobility, specifically focusing on Secure OTA Updates for SDVs — connecting directly to the lab's Stateless Authentication (ZKP–PUF) and Data Sovereignty pillars.

Stateless Resilience: Secure & Autonomous Rollback for Post-Quantum SDV OTA Updates

Active
MSCA Seal of Excellence Inria · SERENDIPITY Team SPRITZ · Univ. of Padua

A MSCA SoE funding extended work in collaboration with SERENDIPITY team, Inria, Lille and SPRITZ Security & Privacy Research Group, Univ. of Padua. The objective is to design a hardware-anchored, constant-time O(1) protocol for fleet-wide version integrity and recovery — it contributes to the lab's Autonomous Rollback & State Integrity pillar.

Lead Dr. Shrikant Tangade
Supervisors Dr. Valeria Loscri
Head of SERENDIPITY Team (Inria Lille, France)

Prof. Mauro Conti
Full Professor (Univ. of Padua, Italy & Orebro Univ., Sweden)
Host Inria Lille – Nord Europe, France
Duration 4 Months (Aug. 2027 - Dec. 2027)
